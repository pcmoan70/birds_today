# European Birding Books Likely Out of Copyright

Conservative rule of thumb for Norway/EU: copyright normally lasts for the creator's life plus 70 years after the end of the death year. In 2026, creators who died in 1955 or earlier are generally safe, but later editions, introductions, translations, restored plates, scans, or database terms may have separate rights.

## Best places to search and download

| Repository | Notes |
|---|---|
| Biodiversity Heritage Library | Best source for old biodiversity literature; often gives volume-level download links and copyright status. |
| Internet Archive | Useful for full-book scans and multiple editions. |
| Project Runeberg | Very useful for Scandinavian public-domain literature. |
| National libraries | Useful for country-specific scans, but check reuse terms. |

## Caveat

The original book may be public domain, but a modern edition, new foreword, new translation, restored plates, OCR correction, or scan/database terms can add separate restrictions. For reuse in an app or website, prefer sources explicitly marked public domain or not in copyright.
