About Birds Today

Birds Today shows the birds you're most likely to meet at your location right now.

How it works

  • It detects your location (or pick one on the map 📍) and uses the BirdNET geographic model to estimate, for each week of the year, how likely every species is to occur there.
  • Residents sizes each bird by how likely it is this week. Migration shows only species arriving now, sized by how sharply their occurrence is rising.
  • Hover a bird for its name, the source of its picture, and how likely it is this week. Click a bird to open its photos and sounds on the Macaulay Library.
  • Use 👍 / 👎 to tell us how good an illustration is.

Where the images come from

  • Most are hand-coloured plates from two public-domain works, via the Internet Archive: John Gould — The Birds of Europe (1837) and H. E. Dresser — A History of the Birds of Europe (1871–1881).
  • The Gould / Dresser / AI switch chooses the source. Species without a historical plate fall back to an AI-generated field-guide illustration.
